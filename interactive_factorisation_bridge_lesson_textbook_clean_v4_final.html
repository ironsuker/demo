Part 1 — Start from Expansion

We already know how to expand brackets. Let’s start with:

\((x+2)(x+9)\)
\[ (x+2)(x+9)=x^2+9x+2x+18=x^2+11x+18 \]

Part 2 — Discover the Fast Method

Example

\((x+2)(x+9)\)
Sum:2+9=11 Product:2×9=18

Result

\(x^2+11x+18\)

The coefficient of \(x\) comes from the sum.

The constant term comes from the product.

中文点睛：一次项系数来自两个常数的和，常数项来自两个常数的积。

General Rule for Expansion

If we expand \((x+p)(x+q)\):

\[ (x+p)(x+q)=x^2+qx+px+pq=x^2+(p+q)x+pq \]

Coefficient of \(x\)

\(p+q\)

sum of the two constants

Constant term

\(pq\)

product of the two constants

Part 3 — Reverse the Process

Expansion

\((x+6)(x+12)=x^2+18x+72\)

Factorisation

\(x^2+18x+72=(x+6)(x+12)\)
Expansion removes brackets.
Factorisation puts brackets back.
中文：因式分解就是把展开结果重新写成括号相乘的形式。

How to Factorise \(x^2+bx+c\)

We reverse the expansion rule.

\[ x^2+bx+c=(x+p)(x+q) \]

Need 1

\(p+q=b\)

sum equals the coefficient of \(x\)

Need 2

\(pq=c\)

product equals the constant term

Worked Example 1

Factorise: \(x^2+7x+12\)
Need two numbers: sum \(=7\), product \(=12\).
\(3+4=7,\quad 3\times4=12\)
\[ x^2+7x+12=(x+3)(x+4) \]

Worked Example 2: Both Numbers Negative

Factorise: \(x^2-8x+12\)

We need two numbers whose sum is \(-8\) and product is \(12\).

Product is positive → same sign. Sum is negative → both numbers are negative.

\[ -2+(-6)=-8,\quad (-2)(-6)=12 \]
\[ x^2-8x+12=(x-2)(x-6) \]

Worked Example 3: Mixed Signs

Factorise: \(x^2-6x-16\)

We need two numbers whose sum is \(-6\) and product is \(-16\).

Product is negative → opposite signs. Sum is negative → the negative number has the larger absolute value.

\[ -8+2=-6,\quad (-8)(2)=-16 \] \[ x^2-6x-16=(x-8)(x+2) \]

Interactive Check: Choose the Pair

Factorise: \(x^2+14x+24\)

Think Further

Can we factorise this expression?

\[ 2x^2+14x+20 \]
\[ 2x^2+14x+20=2(x^2+7x+10) \]
\[ x^2+7x+10=(x+5)(x+2) \]
\[ 2x^2+14x+20=2(x+5)(x+2) \]

First take out the common factor, then use the sum-product pattern.
